The Potato Flake Production Process

At ShivShree Food, we transform carefully selected potatoes into high-quality dehydrated flakes. Our streamlined process‚Äîfrom receiving and cleaning to drying, milling, and packaging‚Äîensures consistent taste, texture, and global-standard quality for every batch.

Receiving & Cleaning

Potatoes arrive from trusted farms or storage bunkers and undergo thorough washing, brushing, peeling, and inspection to ensure only the finest tubers proceed to processing.

Peeling

Potatoes are gently peeled using controlled steam or mechanical systems, preserving the flesh. Peel waste is repurposed sustainably for animal feed or compost.

Steam Cooking

Peeled potatoes are steamed under controlled conditions to gelatinize starch evenly, locking in flavor and nutrients while preparing them for consistent mashing.

Mashing

Cooked potatoes are mashed into a smooth, lump-free texture. Specialized equipment ensures uniformity and optimal moisture for high-quality flake production.

Drying

The potato mash is spread in thin, uniform layers onto heated drums. Controlled dehydration forms delicate flakes while preserving flavor, color, and consistency.

Milling & Sieving

Rough flakes are milled and sieved for uniform particle size and bulk density. Optional optical sorting ensures only premium-quality flakes proceed to packaging.

QC & Sustainability

Every batch undergoes rigorous testing to verify moisture, water activity, and hygiene. Peels and residues are repurposed, ensuring zero waste and eco-friendly operations.

Packaging & Dispatch

The final flakes are packed in food-grade, moisture-proof bags and dispatched globally. Climate-controlled storage ensures premium quality from plant to customer.
